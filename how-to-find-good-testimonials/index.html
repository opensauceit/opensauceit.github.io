How to find good testimonials

opensauce
2020-08-09
advice, product

Testimonials for your product: We need a hero!

Before I jump into talking about what and why you need a person to recommend your product, let me ask you a question: Who is your hero?
That’s a question you don’t ask yourself every day, is it?

To me a hero is a person you look up to: whether it is your aunt, teacher or a celebrity, a hero is someone that inspires us to do better things in life.

The reason why other people ask - “Who is your hero ?” is because they want to know more about you.

Thus, if you pick a hero that is also a public figure you have an advantage. Not many people know about your aunt.

Why they ask?

Understanding who you identify as “your hero” reveals more about your personality that you’d imagine: your values, traits you admire in people, thoughts you resonate with, your political/social/economical/etc. mindset, and general attributes that you care for.

Another fact is that most people aspire to be more like their personal heroes.

If you aren’t aspiring, then you’re probably picking the wrong hero.

For example, if you pick Elon Musk as your hero, I could deduct that you think a lot about new technology. You admire tech, how it can empower human kind. to do better! I can also guess that you appreciate snarky comments and have a pretty bleak vision of the future; but you’re willing to fight for our future with all the resources you can throw at it. (I picked Elon Musk as an example)

Ultimately, there are probably hundreds of thousands of people who consider Elon Musk their hero: they all look up to him, follow his moves and believe in him.

If you look up to Elon Musk, you probably trust him.

Do you trust a random person on the internet? I don’t.
A hero has a special place in your heart. They are a person you trust. Therefore, if your hero says it’s safe to go to Mars by boarding their spaceship, you’re most likely to jump in one, and make your way to Mars.

About your business’ testimonials

Likewise, in business it’s important that you find a hero. A hero can help you by giving a huge boost to your business.
Let’s assume you’re in the business of making custom-fit spacesuits for long-travel.
That is why a lot of people put testimonials on the homepage of their product.

That is why you should refrain from just featuring anybody who says something good about your product on your page.

Let me tell you this: nobody knows about Susan N. or Mike V. that said that your product is cool and really comfy.
Instead, you need an Elon Musk or someone trusted by the audience in your business to say good things about your products.

Ultimately you might not be able to get Elon to rep your product, but there are plenty of amazing Nasa scientists out there that are more willing to try your product and leave you a good testimonial!

Filtering out testimonials: Stuff that won’t help you convert customers

Q. Which testimonials should go on your homepage?

A. The people whose testimonials end up on your homepage should be from the most influential people you can find. They must be known by the audience that you want to convert to customers. That is because Susan N. and Mike V. are hard to relate with, for a new customer, but everyone knows about NASA or Elon Musk.

It is not simple feat to gather a testimonial from a person such Elon Musk, but there are a lot of other people out there that you can reach out to, asking for a testimonial.

Q. That’s right! I can’t possibly have Elon Musk as a testimonial, how do you find testimonials?

A. Here’s what I do:

  1. Create a list of people who you think would be a perfect testimonial: make sure these people are in your industry (for the product you’re selling) and are reachable within the limits of your network.
  2. Analyze the network and reverse engineer a path to them: using tools like LinkedIn you can see how many degrees of separation you are away from being able to reach out to the person whose testimonial you want.
  3. Contact them (template at the bottom of this post) and have them test your tool: whether it’s over a videocall, or giving them a direct sign-up link, maybe even a test account for them to try out without signin-up.
  4. Schedule/Send your invitation to, follow-up, and if nothing happens, rinse and repeat with another person from the list you compiled in 1
  5. Offer them a back-link to say thanks: even if you’re a small website, it still counts.

And that’s all you need to know about how you can also now get testimonials for your landing page.

☢️ Danger zone - Can you fake it till you make it?

A lot of people could simply decide to fake a testimonials, sure that works too - but if the author of the testimonial finds out you’ve lied about the testimonial, you might end up in a sea of trouble! Double think your choices!
